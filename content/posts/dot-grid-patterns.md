# The Art of Dot Grid Patterns

Dot grids are one of those design elements that seem simple at first glance but hide surprising depth.

## The Basics

A dot grid is just a regular array of circles. But the magic happens in the details:

- **Spacing** — Tight grids feel dense and energetic; loose grids feel airy and calm
- **Size** — Tiny dots recede; larger dots become focal points
- **Color** — Subtle grays blend into the background; bold colors demand attention

## Interaction Design

The real power of dot grids emerges when you add interaction. Move your mouse near the dots and watch them react:

- **Bulge effect** — dots push away from the cursor smoothly
- **Repulsion** — dots scatter with physics-based movement
- **Sparkle** — occasional dots flash for visual interest

These micro-interactions transform a static pattern into something alive.

## When to Use Them

Dot grids work best as:

1. **Background texture** — subtle enough to not distract from content
2. **Section dividers** — create visual rhythm between content blocks
3. **Loading states** — animated dots can communicate waiting gracefully

Keep it minimal. Less is always more with generative patterns.
